Notice

Flood-iction is a tool which is intended for use to prepare for flooding.
It is not a tool which provides real-time updates in the event of flooding.
For real-time updates during flooding, use appropriate government and council resources for live updates and instruction.
Currently, Flood-iction is only capably of analysing flooding that results from the Brisbane River overflowing.

1. Enter Location into the Risk Calculator

Use the Risk Calculator page to enter a location.


You can either attempt to search an address or use the provided map to find and click on the location.

2. View Flood Risk Map

Once you have entered a valid locaiton, you will be presented with a results page. This page contains an overall flood risk score as well as additional data to help you understand the flood risk.


The first is a map. This map visualizes the flood risk at and around your location for a flood height of 1 meter. This can be used to note what is and isn't likely to flood, such as roads, or pathways.

3. View Additional Information

In addition to the map, Flood-iction provides a more specific breakdown about the flood risk at various water heights.


This table can help anticipate how much flood water to expect. Decisions such as where best to store belongings to avoid damage, or whether or not performing renovations to raise a building can benefit from this information.

4. Make a Flood Plan

Equipped with some specific flood risk knowledge, it is time to prepare.
The Resources page contains additional flood resources provided by the council and government.
